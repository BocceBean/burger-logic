JBob's Lunch Logic*

Procuring microwavable cheeseburgers from gas stations on the way to work in the morning, then preparing and eating them for lunch is logically superior to other lunch options.

  • Microwavable cheeseburgers can be purchased from many gas stations.
    • Purchasing food on the way to work eliminates the need to prepare food in advance.
    • Multiple outlets for food procurement supports food acquisition even when traffic conditions necessitate route changes.

  • Gas station cheeseburgers are usually chilled, grocery store cheeseburgers tend to be frozen.
    • Chilled cheeseburgers take less time to microwave than frozen cheeseburgers.
    • Shorter cooking times use less energy to attain optimal serving temperature.
    • Gas station cheeseburgers are more energy efficient than the frozen ones sold in grocery stores.
    • Chilled cheeseburgers may be consumed without being heated first in emergencies.
    • Gas station cheeseburgers facilitate emergency preparedness better than their frozen counterparts.

  • Microwave cheeseburgers have essentially no flavor.
    • Having no flavor means they are very unlikely to taste bad.
    • (That there is real logic.)

  • Microwave cheeseburgers need ketchup.
    • Ketchup tastes good.**
    • Putting ketchup on microwave cheeseburgers makes them taste like ketchup.
    • Since ketchup tastes good, putting it on microwave cheeseburgers makes the cheeseburgers taste good.
    • Ketchup tastes essentially the same cold as warm.
    • If a microwave cheeseburger is prepared with ketchup and gets cold, it still tastes good.***

  • Microwave cheeseburger preparation is an efficient process.
    • Microwave cheeseburgers can be cooked in 40-50 seconds.
    • (Application of the aforementioned ketchup does add to prep time, but the risk:reward ratio is favorable.
    • Microwave ovens are often located in close proximity to available, refrigerated cans of Diet Mt. Dew.
    • Therefore cheeseburger prep can incorporate beverage procurement with minimal additional overhead.

  • Microwave cheeseburgers facilitate multitasking and adaptive accommodation.
    • Microwave cheeseburgers can be eaten with one hand.
    • The free hand (either left or right) is remains available to use for typing, mousing, or other one-handed activities.
    • No additional adaptation is required to enable eating microwave cheeseburgers using the left hand.

  • Microwave cheeseburgers can add usable time back to the work day.
    • Deciding what to eat can be time consuming, especially if the menu varies from day to day.
    • Eating microwave cheeseburgers as a matter of course requires no additional thought.
    • Decision-making time that would have been consumed by eating non-microwave cheeseburger foods is automatically reclaimed.
    • Reclaimed decision-making time offsets both ketchup application and Diet Mt. Dew procurement times.
    • Eating microwave cheeseburgers results in a net positive return of time available for work tasks.

  • Microwave cheeseburgers require microwaving.
    • Having food that requires heating by default mitigates the problem of frozen cheese that can occur in non-microwaved sandwiches.
